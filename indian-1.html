One Pan Coconut Chettinad Prawn Curry



One Pan Coconut Chettinad Prawn Curry

YouTube Video

Yield: 4 servings
Servings: 4 plates
Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 20 min
Ready In: 35 min


Cuisine: Indian Course: Main Course Skill Level: Difficult

A street hawker style Indian curry with prawns and rice that's quick and easy. Try this one-pot Indian Coconut prawn curry made with authentic spices using Street Kitchen kits. Cook with prawns and rice in one pan for a dinner that's ready in just 30 minutes.


Ingredients

  • Prawns: 500g
  • Coconut Milk: 400ml
  • Onions: 2 medium
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece
  • Garlic: 4 cloves
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Spices: (cumin, coriander, turmeric, pepper)
    ~ 2 tablespoons total

Step By Step Methods

Place prawns in a bowl and add the ginger & garlic paste from the Street Kitchen pack. Stir to coat.

Heat 1 tbsp of the oil in a large non-stick lidded frying pan over medium high heat. Add the prawns and cook, stirring, for 2-3 minutes or until lightly coloured. Transfer to a plate.

Heat remaining oil in the pan and add the Street Kitchen spices. Cook for 1-2 minutes or until aromatic. Add the tomatoes and cook over high heat for 2 minutes or until starting to split.

Pour in the curry sauce and bring to a simmer. Stir in 1 cup of water and the coconut milk. Add the rice, stir to combine, cover and cook for 10 minutes or until the rice is almost cooked.

Return prawns and chilli to the pan, gently stir to combine. Cover and cook for a further 2 minutes or until the rice and prawns are cooked.

Serve the prawns and rice, scattered with remaining chilli, coriander, and lime wedges.



Tips & Variations with Additional Tips

  1. Tips

    • Fresh Ingredients: Use fresh prawns for the best flavor. Thaw and dry frozen prawns before cooking.
    • Even Coating: Ensure prawns are evenly coated with ginger & garlic paste.
    • Cook Prawns Carefully: Lightly color the prawns initially to avoid overcooking later.
    • Spices: Toast spices in oil to release flavors, but avoid burning them.
    • Tomatoes: Use ripe tomatoes and cook until they start to split for richer flavor.
    • Rice Cooking: Check rice towards the end, adding water if necessary.
    • Balancing Flavors: Adjust seasoning with salt, lime juice, or more chilli to taste.

  2. Variations

    • Vegetables: Add bell peppers, peas, spinach, or zucchini for extra nutrition.
    • Protein Alternatives: Substitute prawns with chicken, tofu, or paneer.
    • Spice Level: Adjust chilli to your heat preference.
    • Coconut Milk: Use full-fat for richer curry or light coconut milk/broth for a lighter version.
    • Herbs: Try fresh basil or mint instead of coriander.
    • Grains: Use quinoa, couscous, or cauliflower rice instead of regular rice.
    • Tanginess: Add tamarind paste or lemon juice for a tangy twist.
    • Nuts: Garnish with toasted cashews or almonds for crunch.
    • Creamy Texture: Stir in plain yogurt or cream at the end.

  3. Additional Tips

    • Serving: Serve the curry with warm naan bread or roti for a complete meal.
    • Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat gently to avoid overcooking the prawns.
    • Marinade: Let the prawns marinate in the ginger & garlic paste for 15-30 minutes for enhanced flavor.

Enjoy experimenting with your prawn curry and make it your own with these tips and variations!
