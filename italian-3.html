Spaghetti with Smoky Tomato & Seafood Sauce



Spaghetti with Smoky Tomato & Seafood Sauce

YouTube Video

Yield: 4 servings
Servings: 4 plates
Prep Time: 5 min
Cook Time: 15 min
Ready In: 45 min


Cuisine: Italian Course: Main Course Skill Level: Easy

Spaghetti with Smoky Tomato & Seafood Sauce: Chilli, fennel seeds and smoked paprika add warmth and flavour to a rich tomato sauce, served with seafood in this quick pasta dish.


Ingredients

  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 red chilli, deseeded and finely sliced
  • 1 ½ tsp fennel seeds
  • 400g spaghetti
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 x 400g cans chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 400g pack frozen mixed cooked seafood, defrosted
  • small bunch parsley or basil, chopped

Nutritions per Serving (1 cup or about 240g)

  • Calories: 638 kcal
  • Fat: 19g
  • Saturates: 3g
  • Carbs: 80g
  • Sugars: 11g
  • Fibre: 7g
  • Protein: 35g
  • Salt: 0.7g


Step By Step Methods

Boil the kettle and heat the oil in a large, deep frying pan. Add the garlic, chilli and fennel seeds, and sizzle for a few mins. Pour the boiling water into a large pan and cook the pasta following pack instructions.

Add the paprika, tomatoes, sugar and seasoning to the pan and simmer for 8-10 mins while the pasta cooks.

Drain the pasta 1 min before the end of the cooking time, reserving a cup of the water. Add the pasta to the sauce with the seafood. Simmer for 1-2 mins, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water if it looks too thick. Toss the pasta through the sauce as it cooks. Add the herbs and black pepper, then serve.



Tips & Variations with Additional Tips

  1. Tips

    • Fresh Seafood: Use fresh seafood for the best flavor. If using frozen seafood, ensure it is fully thawed and patted dry before cooking.
    • Smoky Flavor: Use smoked paprika or chipotle peppers to achieve a smoky flavor.
    • Tomatoes: Use ripe tomatoes or high-quality canned tomatoes for a rich, flavorful sauce.
    • Seafood Cooking: Add the seafood at the end to prevent overcooking and becoming tough.
    • Pasta Cooking: Cook the spaghetti al dente as it will continue to cook slightly when mixed with the sauce.
    • Reserve Pasta Water: Save some pasta cooking water to adjust the sauce's consistency when mixing with the pasta.
    • Layer Flavors: Sauté garlic and onions until fragrant before adding tomatoes and spices.
    • Finish with Herbs: Fresh parsley or basil can be added at the end for a burst of freshness.

  2. Variations

    • Spicy: Add red pepper flakes or fresh chili for a spicy kick.
    • Vegetable Additions: Include sautéed bell peppers, zucchini, or spinach for extra nutrition.
    • Different Pasta: Use linguine, fettuccine, or penne instead of spaghetti.
    • Herb Infusion: Experiment with different herbs like thyme, oregano, or basil.
    • Creamy Sauce: Add a splash of cream or a dollop of mascarpone for a creamy texture.
    • White Wine: Deglaze the pan with white wine for an extra depth of flavor.

  3. Additional Tips

    • Prep Ahead: Chop all vegetables and prepare seafood before starting.
    • Taste and Adjust: Taste frequently and adjust seasoning as needed.
    • Presentation: Garnish with fresh herbs and a drizzle of olive oil before serving.
    • Leftovers: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Reheat gently to avoid overcooking the seafood.
    • Wine Pairing: Pair with a crisp white wine like Sauvignon Blanc or a light red like Pinot Noir.

exploring the variations, you can create delicious Creamy Tomato Risotto that are flavorful, Tangy, satisfying, and versatile.
